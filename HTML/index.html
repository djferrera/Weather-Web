LATITUDE EFFECTS THE WEATHER?

Weather data from over 500 randomly selected cities was analyzed to look at the correlation of equator proximity on a variety of weather facets.

It must be prefaced that all data is somewhat skewed as it was generated on August 16th, while the Northern Hemisphere was at the height of summer and the Southern Hemisphere was in the dead of winter. Had data from mid December been utilized, it is expected that Northern and Southern results would be reversed.

Results were as expected. Temperatures rise as you get nearer to the equator. Within 20° (both north and south) of the equator, the majority of temperatures average between 60° and 90°F. Additionally, equator proximity has varying effects on humidity, wind speed and cloudiness.

Also worth noting, the randomized data appears to account for more cities located in the Northern Hemispehe than the Southern Hemisphere. Studies show that the majority of the world’s population resides in the North (some stating as much as 90%), explaining this geographics disparity.
